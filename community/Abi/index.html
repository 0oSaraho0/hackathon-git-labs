Hi there,

I'm Abi, a trombonist from London in the UK, and a Software Development Engineer in the Front End Core Services Team with giffgaff, a UK-based mobile company.

I've discovered a love of the accessible web - A must for any developer! I love learning about how to code for those with accessibility requirements, and teaching others the things that I've learnt.

If you're interested, feel free to take a look at a few of the things I've done:

  • My GitHub
  • A webinar about the basics of accessibility and meta tags
  • A blog post about accessible buttons
  • A blog post about Top Fullstack Developer Tools for Code Institute
  • A series of blog posts, called the A11y Series, which I wrote for Code Institute :
    • Accessibility in Code
    • Colour & Accessible Websites
    • Alt Text with Images
    • Semantic HTML

When I'm not coding, I'm normally playing music. With so many instruments in my house, it's a shock to find it quiet in here, and when I'm out, I'm normally at a gig. Most of those gigs I'm still working hard, as trombonist in the ska punk band Lead Shot Hazard .

On a race weekend, you'll find me watching the F1, cheering on my favourite driver - Daniel Ricciardo. Unfortunately it seems that my cheering only makes him drive slower...

In my free time, I like to chill out on the sofa with a good game. I'm currently playing through Stardew Valley, which is delightfully cute. I'm also a massive Halo fan, and I'm terrible at racing games (much to my partner's disappointment)!
